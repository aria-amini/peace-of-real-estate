THE PEACE PACT

PURPOSE

This Commitment is designed to reinforce ethical, transparent, and
consumer‑first real estate practices consistent with the National Association of
REALTORS® (NAR) Code of Ethics, particularly Article 1, and with current NAR
Consumer Guides addressing buyer and seller choice, steering, and commission
negotiations.

This document is intended to be signed by the real estate professional and the
consumer as a clear statement of the parties' rights, duties, responsibilities,
and shared expectations.

I. CORE ETHICAL PRINCIPLE (NAR Code of Ethics – Article 1)

Article 1 of the NAR Code of Ethics requires REALTORS® to: • Protect and promote
the interests of their client; • Treat all parties honestly; and • Place the
client's interests above their own.

II. THE PEACE OF REAL ESTATE COMMITMENT

I commit to conducting my real estate services in a manner that promotes
clarity, trust, and peace of mind for consumers.

Specifically, I commit to the following principles:

1. Client Interests First I will protect and promote my client's interests with
   loyalty, care, and diligence, while treating all parties honestly and fairly.

2. Consumer Choice and Autonomy I affirm that: • Buyers and sellers always
   retain the right to make their own decisions; • Consumers are never required
   to accept or offer any particular commission, compensation structure, service
   level, or transaction term; and • All decisions belong to the consumer, not
   the agent, brokerage, multiple listing service(s), or any third party.

3. No Steering Based on Compensation I will not: • Steer buyers toward or away
   from properties based on the presence, absence, or amount of compensation
   offered to me; • Encourage sellers to structure compensation in a way that
   restricts buyer access or choice; or • Allow my personal compensation to
   influence the properties shown, recommended, or discussed.

4. Transparent and Honest Communication I commit to: • Clearly explaining
   available options regarding representation, services, and compensation before
   and during the establishment of an agency relationship; • Answering questions
   truthfully and completely; • Providing no misleading statements, omissions,
   or pressure tactics; and • Ensuring consumers understand that compensation is
   negotiable, not set by law, and must be mutually agreed upon in writing.

5. Respectful Negotiation of Compensation I acknowledge that: • Commissions are
   not fixed, standard, or required by law; • Compensation is a matter for open
   discussion and negotiation between the consumer and the agent; • Compensation
   agreements must be established in writing as required by law and/or NAR; •
   Different properties and transactions may warrant different compensation
   structures; and • A consumer's decision regarding compensation will not
   affect the quality, scope, or professionalism of the services I provide
   unless clearly agreed to in writing.

III. CONSUMER RIGHTS SUMMARY (Based on NAR Consumer Guides)

As a consumer, you have the right to: • Choose whether to work with a real
estate agent; • Choose the type and level of services you receive; • Negotiate
agent compensation freely and without pressure; • View any property you are
qualified to purchase, regardless of compensation offered; • Receive objective
information and honest advice; and • Decline any term or service that does not
align with your goals.

No buyer or seller should ever feel coerced, rushed, or restricted in their
options.

IV. ACKNOWLEDGMENT AND COMMITMENT

By signing below, I affirm that I understand and agree to uphold the Peace Pact
in alignment with the NAR Code of Ethics, applicable Consumer Guides, and
governing real estate laws and regulations.

I acknowledge that this document is intended to reinforce trust, transparency,
and ethical conduct, and does not replace agency agreements, disclosures, or
state laws or required forms.
